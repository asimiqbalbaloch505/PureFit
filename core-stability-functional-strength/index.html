Core Stability: Beyond the Sit-up and Crunch

The primary role of your core is not to create movement like a crunch, but to prevent it. True core strength is defined by Proximal Stiffness, which allows for distal athletic movement. By mastering the ability to Resist Unwanted Movement, you create a rigid cylinder that protects your spinal cord and enhances power transfer between your legs and upper body.

The Science of Anti-Extension and Anti-Rotation

Effective training must challenge your Transverse Abdominis and obliques to stay rigid under tension. Instead of spinal flexion, we focus on stability patterns:

The Natural Weight Belt: Intra-Abdominal Pressure

During heavy squats or deadlifts, your core creates Intra-Abdominal Pressure (IAP). Think of this as an internal airbag that supports your vertebrae. By using Bracing (not just 'sucking it in'), you prevent the load from crushing your spinal discs. This is why a functional core is your best defense against lower back pain.

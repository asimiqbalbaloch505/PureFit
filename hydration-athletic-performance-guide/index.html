Hydration and Athletic Performance: More Than Just Water

Category: Performance Science • 5 min read

Hydration is often overlooked, but it has a profound impact on performance. Even a 2% drop in Intracellular Fluid can lead to a significant decrease in strength and focus.

The Physiology of Plasma Volume

Dehydration decreases your Plasma Volume, making blood thicker. This forces your heart to work harder, leading to higher perceived exertion and poor Osmoregulation.

  • Joint Lubrication: Vital for synovial fluid during heavy lifts.
  • Cognitive Function: Prevents brain fog and maintains coordination.
  • Metabolic Waste: Necessary to flush out lactic acid after intense training.

The Importance of Electrolyte Homeostasis

Water alone isn't enough for high-intensity athletes. You must maintain Electrolyte Homeostasis by replacing Sodium, Potassium, and Magnesium lost through sweat.

Hydration Monitoring Guide

Marker Ideal State Action Needed
Urine Color Pale Straw / Lemonade Maintain Intake
Thirst Level None Drink 250ml every hour
Morning Weight Stable Hydrate if >1kg drop

Don't wait until you're thirsty—stay ahead of the curve to ensure your body is always prepared to perform at its full potential!

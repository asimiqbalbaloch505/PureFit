Full Body Bodyweight Routine: Master Your Own Resistance

Category: Workout Guides • 7 min read

Building a strong, functional physique doesn't require a gym membership. The most effective strength-building tools are your own limbs and gravity. By mastering Calisthenics, you develop a solid foundation of power and body control.

The Pillars of Bodyweight Strength

To maximize muscle growth without weights, focus on the Kinetic Chain and maintain high Time Under Tension. Essential movements include:

  • The Air Squat: The king of lower-body power. Focus on heels to engage glutes.
  • The Classic Push-Up: A total upper-body builder for chest and triceps.
  • Walking Lunges: Excellent for balance and unilateral leg strength.

The Full Body Circuit

Perform this routine 3-4 times a week. Focus on slow, controlled movements:

Exercise Sets/Reps
Push-Ups 3 Sets of 12-15
Air Squats 3 Sets of 20
Plank 3 Sets of 45 Seconds
Lunges 3 Sets of 10 per leg

Core Stability & Spine Protection

Stability is as important as movement. The Plank trains your core to resist extension and protect your spine, improving your overall Proprioception (body awareness).

Consistency is the final ingredient. Quality reps lead to incredible transformations over time!

Carbs are Fuel, Not the Enemy: Strategic Energy for Performance

Despite popular "low-carb" trends, science consistently proves that Complex Polysaccharides are essential for high-intensity training. When you consume carbs, your body converts them into glucose, which is then stored in the liver and muscles as Glycogen.

The Physiology of Glycogen Resynthesis

During a workout, your body taps into these glycogen stores to create ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate). Strategic intake ensures that your muscle fibers are constantly saturated with energy, preventing muscle breakdown during long sessions.

Understanding the Glycemic Index (GI)

The Glycemic Index is a ranking of carbohydrates based on how quickly they raise blood glucose levels. mastered the art of GI selection:

  • Low GI (Slow Fuel): Foods like quinoa and oats provide a steady stream of glucose.
  • High GI (Fast Fuel): Foods like white rice or honey cause a rapid Insulin Spike, beneficial post-workout.

Strategic Carbohydrate Timing

By timing your carbs around your workout window, you ensure the energy is used for Performance and Recovery rather than fat storage.

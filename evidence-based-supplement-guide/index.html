Supplementation: What Actually Works and What is Hype?

Category: Supplements • 5 min read

95% of your results come from food, training, and sleep. Evidence-Based Supplementation is the final 5% edge. In an industry worth billions, marketing often outpaces science. To get the most for your money, you must understand Bioavailability—how much of a nutrient your body actually absorbs.

[Image: Infographic showing the 'Results Pyramid' with Supplements at the tiny top tip]

The Gold Standard: Research-Backed Essentials

Only a handful of compounds have a "Grade A" evidence rating. These focus on cellular energy and hormonal support.

Advertisement

  • Creatine Monohydrate: The most studied supplement in history. It saturates intramuscular phosphocreatine stores, directly increasing ATP production for explosive power.
  • Whey Protein Isolate: Essential for hitting your Leucine Threshold—the specific amount of amino acids needed to trigger Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS).
  • Vitamin D3 + K2: Vitamin D acts more like a hormone than a vitamin. When paired with K2, it ensures calcium goes to your bones (increasing Bone Mineral Density) instead of your arteries.
  • Magnesium Bisglycinate: Highly bioavailable form that supports over 300 enzymatic reactions, primarily focusing on muscle relaxation and nervous system recovery.

The Truth About Pre-Workouts

Most pre-workouts are just expensive caffeine delivery systems. While L-Citrulline (for blood flow) and Beta-Alanine (for buffering lactic acid) have merit, many brands use "Proprietary Blends" to hide under-dosed ingredients. Always look for transparent labels.

Stay Active With a Busy Schedule: The Art of Exercise Snacks

Published in Lifestyle • 5 min read

One of the most common barriers to fitness is the belief that you need large blocks of free time. The secret lies in increasing your NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis)—the energy expended for everything we do that is not sleeping, eating, or sports-like exercise.

1. The Power of 'Exercise Snacks'

Short 'exercise snacks' are effective at keeping your metabolism high and managing Insulin Sensitivity. These micro-bursts add up quickly:

  • The 5-Minute Reset: Bodyweight circuits (squats/planks) between meetings.
  • Post-Lunch Brisk Walk: A 10-minute walk to stabilize blood sugar.
  • Stair Climbing: Skip the elevator to instantly boost heart rate.

2. Efficient Movement for Professionals

By focusing on Compound Movements, you get more results in less time. In fast-paced environments like San Francisco or Chicago, staying active is about opportunistic movement.

3. Strategies for Intentionality

Use these Time-Blocking strategies to stay consistent:

  • Standing Desks: Increase daily calorie burn and improve posture.
  • Walking Meetings: Combine productivity with physical movement.
  • The Evening Wind-Down: 10 minutes of stretching to lower Cortisol.

Every movement counts. By being intentional with your time, you can prioritize your health alongside a demanding career.
